About Taniti Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific, spanning less than 500 square miles. Despite its modest size, the island boasts remarkable diversity in its landscapes – from pristine sandy and rocky beaches to a protected harbor, lush rainforests, and a mountainous interior featuring a small active volcano.

Home to around 20,000 indigenous residents, Taniti's economy has traditionally been centered around fishing and agriculture. Recently, however, the island has seen growth in its tourism sector, welcoming visitors from around the world to experience its natural beauty and unique culture.

Essential Visitor Information

Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but also accepts euros and yen. Major credit cards are widely accepted, and several banks offer currency exchange services.

Language

Younger Tanitians often speak fluent English. In rural areas, English is less common, especially among older residents. Our hospital has multilingual staff.

Safety

Violent crime is rare on Taniti. As tourism increases, petty theft has become more common in tourist areas. The island has one hospital and several clinics.
