Transportation Options

Everything you need to know about getting to and around Taniti Island

Getting to Taniti

Air Travel

Arriving by Air

Most visitors arrive to Taniti by air. The island is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. An expansion project is underway to allow larger jets to land within the next few years.

Travel Times from Airport:

  • • Airport to Taniti City: 20 minutes
  • • Airport to Yellow Leaf Bay: 35 minutes
  • • Airport to Merriton Landing: 15 minutes

Cruise Ship

Arriving by Cruise Ship

A small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. If you're arriving via cruise, you'll have approximately 24 hours to explore the island before departure.

Cruise Information:

  • • Docking location: Yellow Leaf Bay
  • • Shore time: Approximately 24 hours
  • • Distance to Taniti City: 10 minutes by shuttle

Transportation Tips

Getting Around Like a Local

  • Taniti City is very walkable - most attractions are within a 20-minute walk of each other
  • Buses are the most economical way to explore the island and are used frequently by locals
  • Biking is popular in Merriton Landing area, which has dedicated bike lanes
  • Many locals are happy to provide directions - younger Tanitians often speak fluent English

Important Information

  • Helmets are required by law when riding bicycles or scooters
  • Driving is on the right side of the road, same as in the United States
  • Public buses do not operate between 11 p.m. and 5 a.m. - plan accordingly for late activities
  • On national holidays, public transportation schedules may be reduced - check with your accommodation

Accessibility Information

Taniti is committed to making transportation accessible to all visitors:

  • All public buses are equipped with wheelchair ramps
  • Wheelchair-accessible taxis available by request
  • Airport offers assistance for travelers with disabilities
  • Most major attractions have accessible pathways

Transportation FAQs

Do I need to rent a car on Taniti?

It depends on your travel plans. If you're staying primarily in Taniti City, public transportation and occasional taxis will suffice. If you plan to explore multiple areas of the island at your own pace, a rental car offers the most flexibility. Many visitors combine public transportation with specific day rentals.

How reliable is the public bus system?

The public buses in Taniti City run on a regular schedule and are generally reliable. They operate from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily. Buses to other parts of the island run less frequently. We recommend checking the latest schedules upon arrival as they may vary by season.

Can I get around Taniti without a car?

Yes, many visitors enjoy Taniti without renting a car. Taniti City is very walkable, and public transportation can take you to major attractions. Taxis are readily available, and many hotels offer shuttle services to popular destinations. Bicycle rentals are also popular for exploring localized areas.

How do I get from the airport to my hotel?

Most hotels offer airport shuttle services (check when booking). Taxis are readily available at the airport ($25 to Taniti City). Some car rental agencies are located near the airport. If you're staying in Taniti City, public buses also run from the airport to the city center approximately every 30 minutes.
